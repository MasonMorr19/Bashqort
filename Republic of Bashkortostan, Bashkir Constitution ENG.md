Republic of Bashkortostan  
Constitution  

Adopted by the Supreme Council of the Republic of Bashkortostan on December 24, 1993.  
With amendments and additions introduced by the Laws of the Republic of Bashkortostan dated November 3, 2000 (No. 94-z), December 3, 2002 (No. 369-z), June 15, 2006 (No. 322-z), September 18, 2008 (No. 23-z), July 13, 2009 (No. 146-z), May 19, 2011 (No. 395-z), June 28, 2012 (No. 545-z), and March 4, 2014 (No. 57-z).

We, the multi-ethnic people of the Republic of Bashkortostan, united by a common destiny on our land, based on the universally recognized right of peoples to self-determination, equality, freedom, and freedom of will,  
taking into account the voluntary joining of the Bashkir people to Russia in the 16th century, the realization of the Bashkir people's right to self-determination, the formation of the Bashkir Autonomous Republic within the RSFSR in 1919 based on the agreement between the Central Soviet Authority of Russia and the Bashkir government about the Bashkir Soviet autonomy, and the 1990 Declaration of State Sovereignty changing the name to the Republic of Bashkortostan,  
relying on the Constitution of the Russian Federation and the Treaty between the Russian Federation and the Republic of Bashkortostan on defining the limits of authority and dividing powers between their state authorities,  
confirming our respect for human and civil rights and freedoms and all peoples' rights,  
aware of our responsibility before current and future generations for our republic,  
striving to ensure a decent life, peace among citizens, and interethnic harmony,  
and through our fully authorized representatives, adopt the Constitution of the Republic of Bashkortostan.

**Part One**

**Chapter 1**  
**FUNDAMENTALS OF THE CONSTITUTIONAL SYSTEM OF THE REPUBLIC OF BASHKORTOSTAN**

**Article 1**  
The Republic of Bashkortostan is a democratic legal state within the Russian Federation, reflecting the will and interests of all its multi-ethnic population. The names "Republic of Bashkortostan" and "Bashkortostan" have equal meaning.  
The statehood of the Republic of Bashkortostan is expressed in its full state power, within its territory and population, system of state authorities, Constitution and laws, as well as its state languages and state symbols, beyond the powers of the Russian Federation and joint authorities with the Russian Federation.  
The status and borders of the Republic of Bashkortostan can only be changed with the consent of the Republic itself.  
The state languages of the Republic of Bashkortostan are Bashkir and Russian.

**Article 2**  
Human beings and their rights and freedoms are the highest value in the Republic of Bashkortostan. Recognizing, observing, and protecting human and civil rights and freedoms is the duty of the Republic as a state.

**Article 3**  
The constitutional system of popular power operates in the Republic of Bashkortostan.  
The people exercise power directly, and through state and local self-government bodies.  
The highest direct expression of popular power is through referendums and free elections.  
State power in the Republic is exercised in accordance with the Russian Federation Constitution and this Constitution.

**Article 4**  
The Republic of Bashkortostan is an equal subject of the Russian Federation.  
Relations between the state authorities of the Republic and those of the Russian Federation are regulated by this Constitution, the Russian Federation Constitution, the Treaty on division of powers, and other agreements.

**Article 5**  
State power in the Republic is exercised on the basis of the separation of legislative, executive, and judicial powers.  
Legislative, executive, and judicial authorities are independent.

**Article 6**  
State power in the Republic is exercised by the State Assembly (Kurultai), the Head of the Republic, the Government, and the courts.  
(As amended by Law No. 57-z of March 4, 2014)

**Article 7**  
Local self-government is recognized and guaranteed in the Republic. It operates independently within its powers.

**Article 8**  
Private, state, municipal, and other forms of property are recognized and equally protected.

**Article 9**  
Land and other natural resources in the Republic are used and preserved as the basis for the life and activities of its multi-ethnic population.  
The management and use of state-owned land, subsoil, water, forests, and other natural resources are regulated by the Republic's laws, in accordance with federal law and agreements between the Republic and the Russian Federation.

**Article 10**  
State property of the Republic is the wealth of its people.  
It includes property of state organizations, cultural and historical values, the Republic’s budget and off-budget territorial state funds, and other property necessary for fulfilling the Republic's duties and functions.  
Management and use of state property are regulated by Republic laws consistent with federal law.

**Article 11**  
The Republic is a social state aiming to create conditions for a decent life and free development of a person.  
Labor and health are protected; guaranteed minimum wages are established; family, motherhood, fatherhood, childhood, persons with disabilities, and elderly citizens are supported; social services are developed; pensions, benefits, and other social guarantees are provided.

**Article 12**  
Ideological diversity is recognized in the Republic. No ideology may be declared state or mandatory.  
Multi-party system is ensured.  
Public associations aimed at forcibly changing constitutional order, provoking social, racial, national, or religious discord, promoting violence or war are not allowed.

**Article 13**  
The Republic is a secular state. No religion may be declared state or mandatory.  
Religious associations are separated from the state and equal before the law.

**Article 14**  
The Constitution of the Republic of Bashkortostan is adopted by the State Assembly or by referendum.  
It has supreme legal force within the Republic's jurisdiction, is directly applicable, and is used throughout the Republic.  
Laws and other normative legal acts must not contradict this Constitution.  
All state authorities, institutions, local self-government bodies, organizations, public associations, officials, and citizens must comply with the Constitution and laws of the Republic.  
Laws must be officially published; unpublished laws are not used. Normative acts related to rights, freedoms, and duties cannot be applied if not officially published.

**Article 15**  
The Republic conducts international and foreign economic relations within its powers.  
It participates in international organizations, and may enter into agreements with foreign administrative-territorial units and governmental bodies, in accordance with federal law.

**Article 16**  
The provisions of this chapter form the basis of the Republic's constitutional system and cannot be changed except by the procedure established in this Constitution.  
No other provisions of this Constitution may contradict these fundamental principles.

**Part Two**

**HUMAN AND CITIZEN RIGHTS, FREEDOMS, AND DUTIES**

**Article 17**  
Human and citizen rights and freedoms in the Republic are recognized and guaranteed according to universally accepted international law principles, the Russian Federation Constitution, and this Constitution.  
Basic rights and freedoms are inalienable and innate to everyone.  
The exercise of rights and freedoms must not violate the rights and freedoms of others.  
Use of rights and freedoms to forcibly change constitutional order, incite racial, national, social, class, religious hostility, violence, or war is prohibited.

**Article 18**  
Human and citizen rights and freedoms are directly effective. They define the meaning and application of laws, activities of state and local authorities, and are ensured by the courts.

**Article 19**  
All are equal before the law and court.  
Equality of rights and freedoms is guaranteed regardless of gender, race, nationality, language, origin, property or official status, place of residence, religion, beliefs, membership in public associations, or other circumstances.  
No one shall suffer due to disability.  
Men and women have equal rights and freedoms and equal opportunities to exercise them.

**Article 20**  
Listing rights and freedoms in this Constitution does not deny or reduce other generally recognized rights and freedoms of humans and citizens.  
No laws may abolish or diminish human and citizen rights and freedoms.  
Rights and freedoms may only be limited by federal law.

**Article 21**  
Temporary asylum in the Republic is granted to foreign citizens and stateless persons according to federal law.

**Article 22**  
Everyone has the right to life. No one shall be arbitrarily deprived of life.

**Article 23**  
Human dignity is inviolable. Nothing can justify its diminishment.

**Article 24**  
Everyone has the right to liberty and personal inviolability.

**Article 25**  
Everyone has the right to privacy, protection of personal and family secrets, honor, and good name.  
Everyone has the right to confidentiality of correspondence, telephone conversations, postal, telegraph, and other communications.

**Article 26**  
No personal information can be collected, stored, used, or disseminated without consent.  
State and local authorities and officials must ensure access to documents and materials directly concerning individual rights and freedoms.

**Article 27**  
Housing is inviolable. No one may enter someone’s home without their consent, except under circumstances established by federal law or court decision.

**Article 28**  
Everyone lawfully in the Republic has the right to move freely, choose residence and place of stay.  
Republic laws may consider permanent residence criteria for additional benefits funded by the Republic.

**Article 29**  
Everyone is guaranteed freedom of thought and speech.  
No one can be forced to express or renounce their beliefs.  
Everyone has the right to seek, receive, prepare, and disseminate information by any lawful means.  
Freedom of the media is guaranteed.

**Article 30**  
Freedom of conscience and religion is guaranteed, including the right to practice any religion alone or with others, or not to practice any religion, to freely choose, possess, and spread religious and other beliefs.  
The Republic promotes tolerance, patience, and respect among believers of different religions.

**Article 31**  
Everyone has the right to identify and indicate their national origin. No one may be forced to do so.

**Article 32**  
Everyone has the right to freely choose and use their native language for communication, education, upbringing, and creativity.

**Article 33**  
Citizens have the right to directly participate in the Republic’s governance, including through their representatives, according to federal and Republic laws.  
They have the right to elect and be elected to state and local authorities and participate in referendums.

**Article 34**  
Citizens have equal opportunities for state and municipal service.

**Article 35**  
Citizens have the right to peaceful, unarmed assembly, meetings, rallies, demonstrations, marches, and pickets as per federal and Republic laws.

**Article 36**  
Everyone has the right to association, including forming trade unions for protection of their interests.  
No one may be forced to join or remain in any association.

**Article 37**  
Everyone has the right to personally appeal to state and local authorities and send individual or collective appeals.

**Article 38**  
Everyone has the right to own property individually or jointly, use, control, and dispose of it.

**Article 39**  
Everyone has the right to freely use their abilities and property for entrepreneurial and other lawful economic activities.

**Article 40**  
Labor is voluntary. Everyone has the right to freely use their labor abilities, choose their profession and type of work.  
Everyone is entitled to work under safe and hygienic conditions, fair pay without discrimination, and protection against unemployment.

**Article 41**  
Everyone has the right to rest.  
Employees have guaranteed working hours, rest and holidays, annual paid leave, and reduced working hours for certain professions and industries.

**Article 42**  
The family, motherhood, fatherhood, childhood, persons with disabilities, and elderly citizens are under protection.  
Marriage is based on free consent and equality of man and woman.  
Parents have equal rights and duties in caring for and educating children.  
Children have equal legal and social protection regardless of parents’ origin or citizenship.  
The Republic ensures care, upbringing, and education for orphaned and deprived children.  
The Republic promotes charity and services related to child upbringing.  
Mature children able to work have an obligation to care for disabled parents.

**Article 43**  
Everyone has the right to health protection and medical care.  
Medical aid in state and municipal institutions is provided free of charge from budget funds, insurance premiums, and other income.  
The Republic finances health programs, develops healthcare systems, promotes physical culture, sports, and ecological and sanitary-epidemiological safety.

**Article 44**  
Everyone has the right to a favorable environment, accurate information about its condition, and compensation for damage caused by violations of ecological rights.

**Article 45**  
Everyone is guaranteed social security according to age, illness, disability, loss of caregiver, child-rearing, and other cases established by law.  
Voluntary social insurance, supplementary social security, and charity are encouraged.

**Article 46**  
Everyone has the right to housing. No one shall be arbitrarily deprived of housing.  
State and local authorities promote housing construction and create conditions to realize housing rights.  
Citizens in need of housing may receive it free or at affordable prices from state, municipal, and other housing funds according to law.

**Article 47**  
Everyone has the right to education.  
Preschool, basic general, and secondary vocational education in state or municipal institutions shall be accessible and free, including study of the Republic’s state languages and peoples’ languages.  
Everyone has the right to free higher education on a competitive basis in state or municipal institutions.  
Basic general education is compulsory. Parents or guardians must ensure children receive it.  
The Republic supports various forms of education and self-education.

**Article 48**  
Everyone is guaranteed freedom of literary, artistic, scientific, technical, and other creative activities and teaching.

**Article 49**  
State protection of human and civil rights and freedoms is guaranteed.  
Everyone has the right to defend their rights and freedoms by all lawful means not prohibited by law.

**Article 50**  
Judicial protection of rights and freedoms is guaranteed.  
Decisions and actions (or inactions) of state and local authorities and officials may be appealed in court.  
If all domestic legal protection measures are exhausted, everyone has the right to appeal to international bodies according to international treaties.

**Article 51**  
The right to qualified legal assistance is guaranteed.

**Article 52**  
Everyone has the right to compensation for damages caused by unlawful actions (or inactions) of state or local authorities or officials.

**Article 53**  
The right to realize one’s national origin is guaranteed.

**Article 54**  
The Republic guarantees equal rights to preserve native languages, free study and development opportunities.  
Conditions for free study of the Republic’s state languages are created.

**Article 55**  
Private property rights are guaranteed.

**Article 56**  
Consumer rights and lawful interests are protected; public activity for their protection is encouraged.

**Article 57**  
The Republic ensures preservation and protection of the historical and cultural heritage of the Bashkir people and other peoples living in the Republic, and promotes culture development.

**Article 58**  
Freedom of activity of public associations is guaranteed.

**Article 59**  
No law may establish or increase responsibility retroactively.  
No one shall be held liable for an act that was not recognized as a violation at the time it was committed.  
If liability is abolished or mitigated after the violation, the new law applies.

**Article 60**  
Everyone in the Republic must respect the Constitution, laws, rights, freedoms, honor, and dignity of others.

**Article 61**  
Everyone must protect the environment, animals, plants, and natural resources.

**Article 62**  
Everyone must pay legally established taxes and fees.  
New laws establishing taxes or worsening taxpayers’ conditions cannot have retroactive effect.

**Chapter 3**  
**ADMINISTRATIVE-TERRITORIAL STRUCTURE AND CAPITAL OF THE REPUBLIC OF BASHKORTOSTAN**

**Article 63**  
The territory of the Republic includes districts, cities of republic significance, and the closed administrative-territorial unit of Mezhgorye city.

**Article 64**  
The Republic includes:  
- Districts such as Abzelil, Alsheyev, Arkhangelsk, Askyn, Aurgazy, Bakaly, Baltas, Balakhtay, Bishbulak, Blagovar, Buzdyak, Boray, Burzyan, Gafuri, Dyuvan, Yermekey, Yansura, Yulayr, Iglin, Ilishev, Kaltasy, Karidel, Karmaskaly, Kiginsky, Kuygy, Krasnokama, Kugarchin, Kushnarenko, Kuyurgazy, Masyetly, Mishka, Miaka, Nuriman, Salavat, Starobash, Sterlitamak, Tatyshevo, Ufa, Fedorovka, Khaybullinsky, Sakmago, Shishma, Sharan;  
- Combined administrative units equal to districts like Baymak district and city, Beloretsk district and city, etc.;  
- Cities of republic significance: Agidel, Kumertau, Neftekamsk, Oktyabrsky, Salavat, Sibay, Sterlitamak, Ufa.

**Article 65**  
The capital of the Republic is Ufa city. The status of the capital is defined by the Republic’s law.

**Chapter 4**  
**LEGISLATIVE POWER**

**Article 66**  
The highest and sole legislative body of the Republic is the State Assembly (Kurultai).  
It acts as the permanent legislative authority.

**Article 67**  
The State Assembly consists of 110 deputies.  
The election procedure is defined by Republic and federal laws.

**Article 68**  
The State Assembly is elected for five years and convenes its first session within 30 days after elections. The Head of the Republic can call sessions earlier.  
The eldest deputy opens and chairs the first session until the Speaker is elected.

**Article 69**  
A citizen of Russia at least 21 years old with active voting rights may be elected deputy.  
The number of deputies working full-time and their working conditions are defined by law.  
Equal opportunities are guaranteed for deputies to fully perform their duties.  
Restrictions on deputies’ activities are set by federal law.  
Deputies must use their status according to law, ethics, parliamentary discipline, and traditions.

**Article 70**  
Deputies have immunity as defined by federal law.

**Article 71**  
The State Assembly’s powers include:  
1) Adopting and amending the Constitution of the Republic;  
2) Regulating legal relations within the Republic’s powers and joint authority with the Russian Federation;  
3) Interpreting laws and overseeing their implementation;  
4) Approving forecasts of socio-economic development;  
5) Approving the budget and reports on its execution;  
6) Setting taxes and fees per federal law;  
7) Approving budgets of extra-budgetary funds and reports;  
8) Controlling budget execution;  
9) Managing state property and controlling its protection;  
10) Defining administrative-territorial structure and its changes;  
11) Deciding on changes to the Republic’s borders;  
12) Approving and terminating treaties;  
13) Setting procedures and dates for referendums;  
14) Setting procedures and dates for State Assembly elections;  
15) Setting procedures and dates for Head elections;  
16) Organizing the Presidium, committees, electing the Speaker;  
17) Considering votes of no confidence in the Head;  
18) Approving the Prime Minister’s appointment;  
19) Appointing Constitutional Court judges;  
20) Agreeing on Prosecutor appointment;  
21) Proposing amendments or repeal of executive acts;  
22) Appealing executive acts in court;  
23) Initiating legislation in State Duma;  
24) Holding parliamentary investigations and hearings;  
25) Appointing and dismissing human rights, child rights, and entrepreneurs’ rights commissioners;  
26) Appointing and dismissing Control and Accounts Chamber officials;  
27) Receiving appeals to Constitutional Courts;  
28) Electing magistrate judges;  
29) Participating in appointment of federal territorial executive heads;  
30) Appointing and dismissing public members of judicial qualification boards;  
31) Appointing members of the Central Election Commission;  
32) Early termination of deputies’ powers;  
33) Setting election procedures for local self-government;  
34) Hearing annual reports from the Head and executive authorities;  
35) Establishing state awards and honorary titles;  
36) Other matters within its powers per federal and Republic laws.  
The State Assembly ensures compliance with the Russian Constitution and federal laws.

**Article 72**  
The State Assembly creates conditions for forming and representing public opinion in legislative processes.

**Article 73**  
Citizens and their associations have the right to express their will and interests through representative democracy and public institutions in forming the legal system.

**Article 74**  
Citizens and associations have initiative rights to submit proposals for adoption, amendment, or repeal of laws, as defined by law.

**Article 75**  
The State Assembly adopts laws and decisions on matters within its competence.

**Article 76**  
Legislative initiative rights belong to deputies, committees, the Head, Government, Constitutional Court, Central Election Commission, Trade Unions Federation, Prosecutor, local self-government, and citizens and associations exercising initiative rights.  
Bills on taxes or financial obligations are considered on the Head’s proposal or with his conclusions within one month.

**Article 77**  
Laws are adopted by majority vote of deputies present, unless federal law states otherwise.  
Decisions are adopted by majority vote of elected deputies present.

**Article 78**  
Adopted laws are sent to the Head within seven days for promulgation.  
The Head must promulgate or veto laws within 14 days.  
If vetoed, the Assembly reconsidered the law. If approved again by at least two-thirds majority, the Head cannot veto and must promulgate within seven days.  
The procedure for promulgating the Constitution is defined by law.

**Article 79**  
The State Assembly establishes the Control and Accounts Chamber. Its composition, powers, and procedures are defined by law.

**Article 80**  
The State Assembly’s powers may be terminated early in cases defined by federal law.  
Extraordinary elections are held per federal and Republic laws.

**Article 81**  
The State Assembly participates in social conflict resolution through social partnership and public dialogue.

**Chapter 5**  
**EXECUTIVE POWER**

**Article 82**  
The Head of the Republic is the highest-ranking official, representing the Republic in relations with the President of Russia, federal authorities, other federal subjects, local authorities, public associations, and in international and economic relations.  
The official title in the Republic’s state languages is equally "Head of the Republic of Bashkortostan" and "Glava Respubliki Bashkortostan."

**Article 83**  
The Head is elected by citizens residing in the Republic with active voting rights, by equal and direct secret ballot.  
Candidates must be Russian citizens aged 30 or older, meeting federal requirements for highest office in a Russian Federation subject.  
The term is five years.  
Restrictions related to duties are established by federal law.

**Article 84**  
The Head starts exercising powers from the moment of taking the oath.

**Article 85**  
Upon assuming office, the Head takes the oath:  
"I swear to faithfully serve the multi-ethnic people of the Republic of Bashkortostan, strengthen and defend its statehood, comply with the Constitution of the Russian Federation and the Republic, and guarantee human and civil rights and freedoms."  
The oath is taken solemnly in the presence of deputies, Government members, and Constitutional Court judges, in the Republic’s state languages.

**Article 86**  
The Head must know the Republic’s state languages.

**Article 87**  
The Head:  
1) Guarantees enforcement of human and civil rights, protects the Republic’s economic and political interests, ensures legality and order;  
2) Defines main directions of socio-economic development;  
3) Forms and leads the Government; accepts Government resignation;  
4) Appoints the Prime Minister with State Assembly consent;  
5) Defines the structure of executive authorities;  
6) Appoints and dismisses deputies and ministers on the Prime Minister’s proposal;  
7) Promulgates or vetoes laws;  
8) Submits draft laws to the State Assembly;  
9) Proposes socio-economic development programs;  
10) Addresses the State Assembly with messages and annual reports;  
11) Participates in State Assembly sessions with advisory vote;  
12) Calls the first and extraordinary sessions of the State Assembly;  
13) Proposes amendments or repeal of Assembly decisions;  
14) Can dissolve the State Assembly early;  
15) Proposes candidates for Constitutional Court judges;  
16) Proposes candidates for magistrate judges;  
17) Proposes candidates for human rights, child rights, and entrepreneurs' rights commissioners and issues related to their dismissal;  
18) Proposes candidates for Control and Accounts Chamber officials;  
19) Organizes the Interdepartmental Public Security Council;  
20) Coordinates executive authorities and their cooperation with federal, regional, local, and public bodies;  
21) Proposes candidates for public members of judicial qualification boards;  
22) Signs treaties and agreements on behalf of the Republic;  
23) Appoints fully authorized representatives to federal and foreign bodies;  
24) Organizes the administration of the Head;  
25) Appoints members of the Central Election Commission;  
26) Awards state honors;  
27) Grants honorary and other titles;  
28) Performs other powers as per Constitution and laws.  
If the Head temporarily cannot perform duties (due to illness or leave), powers are exercised by the Prime Minister unless otherwise provided by federal law.  
The Head must comply with the Russian Constitution, federal laws, decrees of the President of Russia, Government decisions, this Constitution, and Republic laws.

**Article 88**  
Except as provided by federal law, the Head cannot dissolve or suspend elected state or local authorities.

**Article 89**  
The Head’s honor and dignity are protected by law.  
Legal status guarantees are established by federal and Republic laws.

**Article 90**  
The Head’s powers may end early as provided by federal law.  
A temporary acting Head is appointed as per federal law.  
The acting Head may not dissolve the State Assembly, propose constitutional amendments, call referendums, form or dismiss the Government, or exercise certain powers listed in Article 87.

**Article 91**  
The highest executive body is the Government of the Republic.  
The Head leads the Government.

**Article 92**  
The Government is the permanent executive body, ensuring compliance with the Russian Constitution, federal laws, normative acts, the Republic’s Constitution, laws, and other normative acts.  
It is part of the unified executive system of the Russian Federation within the scope of federal and joint powers.  
The Government leads the system of executive authorities of the Republic.

**Article 93**  
Except as provided by federal law, the Government operates during the Head’s term.  
The Government continues to perform duties until the new Government is formed after the Head’s new term begins.

**Article 94**  
The Government is formed by the Head and includes the Prime Minister, Deputy Prime Ministers, ministers, heads of state committees, and other executive bodies’ leaders.  
The Head may include other state officials as members.

**Article 95**  
The newly formed Government presents its program for the term to the Head and the State Assembly.

**Article 96**  
The Government:  
1) Implements measures to protect human and civil rights;  
2) Protects property and public order, fights terrorism, extremism, and crime;  
3) Prepares the budget and socio-economic development programs for submission to the Head and State Assembly;  
4) Reports annually to the Head and State Assembly on activities and budget execution;  
5) Carries out measures for comprehensive socio-economic development and manages economic and social processes;  
6) Participates in unified federal financial policy;  
7) Participates in unified federal policies in culture, science, and education;  
8) Participates in unified federal policies in health, social protection, and welfare;  
9) Participates in unified federal policies in ecology and environmental protection;  
10) Manages state property and federal property assigned to the Republic;  
11) Proposes to local authorities to bring their acts in line with laws or appeal to courts;  
12) Enters into agreements with federal and regional executive bodies;  
13) Conducts foreign economic activities;  
14) Establishes and leads other executive bodies;  
15) Performs other powers as defined by law.

**Article 97**  
The Head issues decrees and orders based on the Russian Constitution, federal laws, President’s decrees, Government decisions, this Constitution, and Republic laws, and controls their implementation.  
The Government issues decisions and orders within its competence and controls implementation.  
Acts of the Head and Government must be enforced within the Republic.  
They must comply with the Russian Constitution, federal laws, presidential decrees, Government decisions, this Constitution, and Republic laws.

**Article 98**  
The Government coordinates the activities of ministries, state committees, and other executive bodies.  
These bodies implement sectoral and intersectoral management.

**Article 99**  
The Government’s powers, composition, structure, work procedures, and relations with other state bodies are defined by federal law and the law on the Government.

**Chapter 6**  
**GUARANTEES FOR EFFECTIVE PUBLIC AUTHORITY IN THE REPUBLIC**

**Article 100**  
Public authorities promote civil society and ensure public participation in governance and local self-government.

**Article 101**  
Relations among public authorities are based on honesty, openness, control over decision-making and implementation, coordination, and mutual responsibility in governance.

**Article 102**  
State authorities promote public safety, internal unity, cultural and linguistic diversity, equality, cooperation, and municipal integration.  
They encourage financial equalization and provide administrative, legal, and investment support to territories implementing local self-government.

**Article 103**  
Disputes between public authorities are resolved by commissions on a parity basis, through reconciliation, consultations, negotiations, or in court.

---

**Chapter 7**  
**JUDICIAL POWER**

**Article 104**  
Judicial power is exercised by the Constitutional Court of Bashkortostan, federal courts, and magistrates.

**Article 105**  
Courts of the Republic include the Constitutional Court and magistrates.  
Their powers, organization, and activities are defined by the Russian Constitution, this Constitution, federal and Republic laws.

**Article 106**  
The Constitutional Court ensures constitutional order and protection of basic human and civil rights by reviewing:  
1) Laws of the Republic;  
2) Normative acts of the State Assembly;  
3) Normative acts of the Head;  
4) Normative acts of the Government and other executive bodies;  
5) Normative acts of local self-government;  
6) Legal agreements between state and local authorities within the Republic;  
Acts declared unconstitutional lose force.  
The Court interprets the Constitution and considers other constitutional-legal matters as provided by law.  
Its decisions are final and not subject to review by other courts.

**Article 107**  
Citizens of Russia meeting federal and Republic criteria may be judges in Republic courts.

**Article 108**  
Court financing must ensure full and independent administration of justice.

---

**Chapter 8**  
**LOCAL SELF-GOVERNMENT**

**Article 109**  
Local self-government is exercised in cities, rural settlements, and other territories considering historical and local traditions.  
Changes to territorial boundaries require public consent.

**Article 110**  
Local self-government ensures independent resolution of local issues, management and use of municipal property.

**Article 111**  
Local self-government is exercised by citizens directly (referendum, elections, other forms) and through elected and other bodies.

**Article 112**  
Local authorities independently manage municipal property, form and execute local budgets, set local taxes and fees, maintain public order, and resolve other local issues.  
Their structure is determined by the people.  
State authorities assist local self-government.

**Article 113**  
Local self-government is protected by judicial review, compensation for additional costs from state decisions, and prohibition of restricting rights defined by laws.

**Article 114**  
Material and financial resources needed to exercise powers may be assigned by law.  
Such powers are controlled by state authorities.

---

**Chapter 9**  
**FINANCES AND BUDGET**

**Article 115**  
The Republic’s budget system is determined by federal and Republic laws.

**Article 116**  
The Republic participates in unified federal policy on finances, budget relations, and taxation within its competence.

**Article 117**  
The budget and off-budget funds are adopted annually by the State Assembly on the Head’s proposal.

**Article 118**  
The Republic establishes taxes and mandatory payments to the budget as per federal law.

---

**Chapter 10**  
**STATE SYMBOLS OF THE REPUBLIC OF BASHKORTOSTAN**

**Article 119**  
The Republic has its own State Emblem, Flag, and Anthem, reflecting statehood, the uniqueness, and traditions of the Bashkir people.

**Article 120**  
The State Emblem depicts the Salavat Yulaev monument against a rising sun encircled by a national ornament.  
Below is a wreath of kurai flowers colored like the flag, with the inscription "Bashkortostan."  
Colors and symbols are described in detail.

**Article 121**  
The State Flag is a rectangular fabric with three equal horizontal stripes: blue (top), white (middle), and green (bottom).  
The white stripe features a golden emblem of a stylized kurai flower with seven petals inside a circle.

**Article 122**  
The State Anthem is a musical and poetic composition. Its music and lyrics are approved by law.

---

**Chapter 11**  
**AMENDMENTS AND ADDITIONS TO THE CONSTITUTION OF THE REPUBLIC OF BASHKORTOSTAN**

**Article 123**  
Proposals to amend the Constitution may be made by:  
- At least one-third of State Assembly deputies;  
- The Head of the Republic;  
- The Constitutional Court.

**Article 124**  
Amendments to Chapters 1 and 11 require support from at least 50,000 voters before consideration.  
Other amendments are adopted by the State Assembly in the usual way.  
The procedure is defined by law.

**Article 125**  
Amendments require a three-reading approval by at least two-thirds of deputies.  
If the Assembly does not pass the amendment within one year of proposal, it is considered rejected and cannot be reintroduced within one year.

**Article 126**  
Changes to Article 64 on administrative-territorial structure are made based on laws establishing new administrative units within the Republic.

---

**Part Two**

**Final and Transitional Provisions**

1. The 1994 Treaty between Russia and the Republic on division of powers remains in force unless it contradicts the Constitutions.  
If contradictions arise, the Constitutions prevail.

2. According to the amended versions of the Constitution, the elected President of the Republic exercises powers until the end of the term.
